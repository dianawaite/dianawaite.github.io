Design Principles

Fitt's Law

Historic Park City

www.historicparkcity.com

Fitt's Law states that the bigger an object is, and the closer it is, the easier it is to use. This page fits this description, because there is a big button right at the bottom of the screen when you open the website. It isn’t completely overpowering, but the red circle does draw your attention to it.

Hick's Law

Ivory Homes

www.ivoryhomes.com

Hick’s Law says that with every additional choice it increases the time required to take a decision. When we have too many choices, we get paralyzed, so we don’t make any decisions. In web design, this means that the website design must not give too many options. On this Ivory Homes home page, I liked that when I first get to the page, there are only 4 options right in front of me.

Proximity

Historic Park City

www.historicparkcity.com

Proximity is how you place items and content on a page to establish a relationship between the items. This page is clean and simple and uses the proximity principle perfectly. The icons are above each item, and the couple of lines of text are grouped together. There is enough space between them so you know that they are separate items.
