Design Principles

Fitt's Law

Historic Park City

Historic Park City Utah

Fitt's Law states that the bigger an object is, and the closer it is, the easier it is to use. This page fits this description, because there is a big button right at the bottom of the screen when you open the website. It isn’t completely overpowering, but the red circle does draw your attention to it. Most websites have been updated for the current time we are in, with the pandemic and the economic impacts. This website wants you to click on this link to easily go to a place to learn how to support small business right now. This website is very clean and classy, and they didn’t want to detract from that with a giant circle filled in with red, so they went a little more subtle, but equally as impactful in my opinion.

Hick's Law

Ivory Homes

Ivory Homes

Hick’s Law says that with every additional choice it increases the time required to take a decision. When we have too many choices, we get paralyzed and can’t make a decision, so we don’t make any decisions. In web design, this means that the website design must be clean and simple, and don’t give too many options. On this Ivory Homes home page, I liked that when I first get to the page, there are only 4 options right in front of me. Normally this seems like the navigation and wouldn’t be proper to have it so prominent on the page. But it works for this site, because each user is coming for something different, and this is a very easy way to navigate them to what they are looking for.

Proximity

Historic Park City

Historic Park City Utah

Proximity is how you place items and content on a page to establish a relationship between the items. When things are closer together, our brains connect the items together into a group. I liked this page, it was clean and simple, and uses the proximity principle perfectly. The icons are above each item, and the couple of lines of text are grouped together. Then there is enough space between them so you know that they are separate items. You don’t have to wonder if the icon goes with the text above or below it, because it is obviously with the text below it.
